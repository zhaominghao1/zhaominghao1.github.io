    • class="project-title" href = "https://github.com/zhaominghao1/A_Dynamical_Analysis_of_College_Admissions" target="_blank" College Admissions and Replicator Dynamics

      In this project, we examine the college admission process through mathematical modeling and game theory. Unlike the classic Gale-Shapley stable matching algorithm described in Roth 1985, which provides a static one-time solution, we explore how colleges dynamically adapt their admission strategies over time based on the outcomes of previous application cycles. Using a simplified model involving two colleges and a continuous population of students, we apply replicator dynamics to capture the evolu- tion of strategic preferences in a competitive environment. The model is governed by a system of ordinary differential equations, where the frequency of a college's strategy increases if its payoff exceeds the average. We analyze the resulting dynamics and investigate conditions under which strategies stabilize or fluctuate. This framework provides insight into how institutional behavior in college admissions may shift over time in response to competition, perceived utility, and feedback from earlier cycles. Our findings suggest that even in a simplified system, dynamic strategy adjustment can lead to nontrivial long-term patterns that mirror real world behavior.
